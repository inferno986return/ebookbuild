Introduction

I remember when I first interacted with an e-book and that was in the summer of 2013. I was on a flight with my family either going to or heading back from New York (JFK) and as a way of keeping myself preoccupied I asked if I could use my sister's Amazon Kindle e-ink e-reader. I was intrigued at the time because I liked how unlike PDF files the reader could interact with the text, they could make it larger or change the font to give themselves a more comfortable reading experience.

In the late summer of 2014 I managed to get myself an apprenticeship in e-book development near Oxford. I was fortunate because I had dropped out of university after admittedly an emotionally turbulent year. There I was taught much that I know today about developing e-books with the vendor-locked, proprietary, in-house software.i The apprenticeship ended a year later but when I left I felt unsatisified, I liked developing e-books and I wanted to find a way to make to process of developing them from scratch available to everyone and that was my motivation for what would later become ebookbuild.

Along with making a freely-available cross-platform e-book creation tool, I wanted to assist prospective users as much as possible by providing several sample projects analyse and making the source code (though not the content) of the e-book available under a public domain license.ii

This book is also one of my sample projects. I liken it to the RPG Maker VX tutorial game RPG Maker VX School as you can learn not just by running the game but also by examining the source code that made it.iii
