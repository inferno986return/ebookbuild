Introduction

I recall first interacting with an e-book in the summer of 2013. I was on a flight with my family either going to or heading back from New York (JFK) and as a way of keeping myself preoccupied I asked if I could use my sister’s e-ink Amazon Kindle. I was intrigued at the time because I liked how unlike PDF files the reader could interact with the text, they could make it larger or change the font to give themselves a more comfortable reading experience.

In the late summer of 2014 I managed to get myself an apprenticeship for an e-book developer and micropublishing house. I was fortunate because I had dropped out of university after admittedly an emotionally turbulent year. There I was taught much that I know today about developing e-books by using their own proprietary, in-house software.i The apprenticeship ended a year later but when I left I felt unsatisfied, I liked developing e-books and I wanted to find a way to make from-scratch e-book development available to everyone without vendor-lockin. This was my motivation for what would later become ebookbuild, a freely-available cross-platform e-book creation tool that anyone can use and modify under the terms of the GNU General Public License.ii

I also wanted to assist prospective users as much as possible by providing several sample projects for study. I have made the source code (though not the content) of these e-books available under a public domain license. This book is also one of my sample projects. I liken it to the RPG Maker VX tutorial game RPG Maker VX Schooliii as you can learn not just by running the game but also by examining the source code that made it.

Hal Motley
