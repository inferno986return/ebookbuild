Chapter 3: What is an E-book?

E-books are digital books. In this day and age most e-books use either the ePub, Mobi or PDF format.1 I personally describe an e-book as "a website in a zipped folder" because both the ePub and Mobi formats use HTML and CSS to format and style each page.

There are two types of e-book:

  • Reflowable - The e-book reader scales the content to the e-reader's screen size. A well designed reflowable e-book looks good on both a 5 inch smartphone and a 12 inch tablet, plus everything in-between.
  • Fixed-layout - The e-book reader renders the e-book with a fixed layout that cannot be changed. This is used for an e-book that is dependent on images...

E-books are read on dedicated software called e-book readers or e-readers.

ePub

The ePublication (or ePub) is an open format, that is as of 2017? maintained by the World Wide Web Commission (W3C) making it an official web standard.2

There are two supported versions of the ePub standard:

  • 3.1 (latest release)
  • 2.0.1 (maintenance release)

The reasoning behind two supported versions is to provide compatibility for older e-readers. However ePub 3 is recommended as it includes more features such as support for mathematical formulae via MathML, right-to-left and vertical text directions for languages such as Arabic and Japanese, as well as improvements to the metadata such as adding book reviews and excerpts.

ebookbuild as of v0.8111 supports only 2.0.1 but I have plans to add addition support for ePub 3 going forward.

Mobi

The Mobipocket (or Mobi) format was adopted for the popular Kindle e-readers when Amazon acquired the company. Mobi e-books are a proprietary format and are different to the ePub, though their popularity cannot be overlooked.

1. While many publications are freely and commericially available in the Portable Document File (PDF) format and labelled as e-books; this book treats them as documents due to their fixed-layout (usually to A4 pages). The fixed-layout can cause an inferior reading experience on small-screens, so for that reason I do not recommend the PDF format - unless you require a file format everyone recognises and a consistent layout is crucial (such as with a magazine).
